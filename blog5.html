Blog 10/2 to 10/8

As October begins, I have continued to expand on my knowledge of Cybersecurity and professionalism through several different meetings and events. Business Symposium provided a great opportunity to refine my interaction skills as well as to network with several professionals not necessarily in my field, but also to gain valuable feedback on how to improve my resume. However, I did happen to interview with Mr. Travis McWaters, a Cyber Threat Hunter at PepsiCo, who provided me with a very useful understanding of how some larger companies approach security and his path getting to where he is today. In addition to this, I also conducted two informational interviews, with Mr. Ryan Carter (Co-founder and VP Security of StackPath) and Mr. Scott Stewart(Security Architect at AWS). Mr. Carter gave me a lot of insight on the current Cybersecurity industry including many of the challenges it faces as they are reflected in his company, StackPath. My interview with Mr. Stewart was very helpful as well, as I gained a deeper understanding of defense vs compliance as well as discussing more technical aspects of security including resources I could use to better understand how systems are actually secured. In addition to both interviews, I also attended a security conference held by General Datatech, called GDT Tech Night. In it, I was able to connect with many professionals currently in the Cybersecurity field, as well as reconnect with Mr. Moez Janmohammad, who I had interviewed earlier in the year. The night mainly discussed CISCO Firepower devices, and although I was unfamiliar with many of the terms used, it helped me raise a lot of questions on what I would like to research next. I look forward in the coming weeks to delve deeper into the technical aspect of systems and network security, as well as progressing in my search for a mentor.
