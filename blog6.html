Blog 10/9 to 10/16

I began this week by completing my Topic Proposal, a roughly one page statement describing why I chose Cybersecurity as my topic of study, how I became interested in it, and what will enable me to be successful pursuing a career in this field. Writing the topic proposal pushed me to deeply consider my journey and motivation to go in to Cybersecurity, as well as focusing my goals and aspirations for the future. In addition to the proposal, I also completed two assessments this week, including a research assessment on cloud computing risks and my first interview assessment, reviewing my informational interview with Mr. William Gordon. I found this assessment to be particularly challenging, as I had to reflect on all the information Mr. Gordon had given me around a month ago. Looking back at my notes for the interview helped in writing the assessment, since Mr. Gordon gave me so much valuable information, but I felt that I still left out some aspects of Cybersecurity that we discussed. The cloud computing risks assessment was one of the most complicated research assessments I had completed so far, using complex industry terminology throughout the publication. I decided to research cloud risks since in every one of the five interviews I have completed, each professional has expressed some extent of concern regarding the security of cloud computing. As I complete more research and interviews, I will continue to research topics prevalent in the current state of the Cybersecurity industry.
