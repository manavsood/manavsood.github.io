Blog 9/4 to 9/10

My third week in the ISM program was full of new experiences. In addition to learning of the attire requirements when meeting for interviews or for professional events, I built a contact list with over 20 professionals in the Cybersecurity field in the DFW area. Although I was initially skeptical of how many people I would be able to find through Linkedin, I was surprised by the substantial volume of cybersecurity industry workers in this area. Now that I have gathered the contact information of these professionals, the next step will be to begin reaching out to them and requesting to conduct an interview about their profession, with questions I also prepared this week. I am anxious to make contact with people working in my field as I anticipate their skepticism for a high school student trying to study such a complex and evolving topic. However, I am eager to schedule and conduct my first interviews as well as further build my network of professionals. Alongside this, I completed my second Research Assessment largely focused on the classification of security threats, which allowed me to gain more in depth knowledge on what Cybersecurity professionals are trying to defend against. I chose to research the various categorizations of threats rather than how systems are secured because I think that to truly understand how to protect a system it will be essential for me to know what exactly is threatening it. I believe that I made significant progress this week both in gaining knowledge in regard to my topic and advancing my professional career. For next week and the future, my goals will be to continue my search for a mentor through interfacing with professionals via scheduled interviews, as well as to transition towards learning more in detail about the technical and business aspects of Cybersecurity.
